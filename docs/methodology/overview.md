# Methodology Overview

The AI Delegation Curve is a composite index estimating AI influence and delegated workflow share across consequential domains of work and civic life.

The score is not a literal claim that a fixed percentage of all human decisions are made by AI. Each domain combines the strongest available public evidence for AI involvement in real workflows, then normalizes those indicators onto a 0-100 scale.

## Measurement Principles

1. Prefer direct workflow evidence over adoption sentiment.
2. Keep source semantics aligned with indicator names.
3. Use proxy evidence only when direct delegated-decision data is unavailable.
4. Separate scored inputs from context, guardrails, and capability benchmarks.
5. Preserve archived published runs while maintaining a comparable public curve series.

## Source Types

Strong source families include:

- transparency reports and public operational disclosures,
- regulatory datasets and official lists,
- public filings,
- representative surveys with clear denominators,
- peer-reviewed or well-specified research studies,
- platform telemetry and market structure reports.

Context sources are still useful when they clarify scope, confidence, denominator risk, or deployment friction. They do not automatically change the score.

## Current Domains

The current index tracks nine domains:

- content moderation,
- algorithmic trading,
- software development,
- customer support,
- credit and lending,
- medical diagnosis,
- legal research and review,
- recruitment and screening,
- education and assessment.

Each domain has its own evidence note under [`docs/evidence/2026-q2`](../evidence/2026-q2/).

## Status Bands

- **Nominal:** below 40
- **Elevated:** 40 to 74.9
- **Autonomous:** 75 and above

The bands are interpretive labels for the composite score, not regulatory or safety classifications.
